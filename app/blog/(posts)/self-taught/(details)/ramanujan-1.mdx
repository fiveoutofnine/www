The number 1729 is known as the [Hardy&mdash;Ramanujan number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1729_(number)) after a famous visit by Hardy to see Ramanujan at a hospital:

<Blockquote source={{ label: 'G.H. Hardy', url: 'https://web.archive.org/web/20120716185939/https://www-gap.dcs.st-and.ac.uk/~history/Quotations/Hardy.html' }}>
  I remember once going to see him when he was ill at Putney.
  I had ridden in taxi cab number 1729 and remarked that the number seemed to me rather a dull one, and that I hoped it was not an unfavorable omen.
  "No", he replied, "it is a very interesting number; it is the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two different ways."
</Blockquote>

The two different ways are:

<MathDisplay className="[&>span]:my-0">
$$
1729 = 1^3 + 12^3 = 9^3 + 10^3
$$
</MathDisplay>
